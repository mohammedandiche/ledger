Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 3, 2026

Ledger is a budgeting app that connects to your self-hosted Actual Budget server. Your financial data belongs to you.

Data We Do Not Collect

We do not collect, store, transmit, or have access to:

  • Your financial data (transactions, accounts, budgets, payees, categories)
  • Your server URL, credentials, or authentication tokens
  • Analytics, usage data, or behavioral tracking
  • Device identifiers for advertising purposes
  • Location data
  • Contacts, photos, or other personal files

Where Your Data Lives

All budget data is stored on your own Actual Budget server. The app communicates directly with your server over HTTPS. We have no servers that sit between you and your data.

Authentication tokens are stored locally on your device using the iOS Keychain (via Expo SecureStore). They never leave your device except when authenticating with your server.

On-Device Storage

The app stores the following locally on your device:

  • A cached copy of your budget database (for offline access)
  • Your server authentication token (in the iOS Keychain)
  • App preferences such as theme selection
  • A write counter for the free tier (in the iOS Keychain)

In-App Purchases

Purchases are processed by Apple through the App Store. Purchase validation is handled by RevenueCat, a third-party service. RevenueCat receives:

  • An anonymous app user ID (not linked to your identity)
  • Your purchase receipt (to verify entitlements)

RevenueCat does not receive any of your financial data. See RevenueCat's privacy policy for details.

Third-Party Services

  • Apple App Store — processes in-app purchases
  • RevenueCat — validates purchase receipts and manages entitlements

No other third-party services, SDKs, analytics tools, or ad networks are used.

Children's Privacy

The app is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new "last updated" date.
